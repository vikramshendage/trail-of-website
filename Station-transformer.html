What is a Station Transformer?

Critical Substation Support Infrastructure

A Station Transformer (also known as an Auxiliary or Station Service Transformer) is a highly reliable electrical machine used within power plants and large utility substations to step down high voltages from the main bus to provide low-voltage auxiliary power.

This power is critical. It feeds the substation’s own localized systems—such as control rooms, protection relays, battery chargers, cooling fans, HVAC, and lighting. If a station transformer fails, the substation may lose its ability to monitor and protect the grid.

Station vs. Power Transformers
  • Function: Power Transformers transmit bulk power across the grid. Station Transformers consume a tiny fraction of that power specifically to keep the substation itself running.
  • Design Priority: Station transformers are engineered for extreme, uninterrupted reliability (often redundant) rather than massive MVA capacity, as the loads they supply are mission-critical control circuits.

Key Advantages

  • Designed for continuous auxiliary duty without interruption.
  • Exceptional short-circuit withstand capabilities.
  • Highly stable voltage regulation for sensitive relay equipment.
  • Customizable secondary voltages for multiple control panels.
  • Suitable for harsh indoor and outdoor environments.

Auxiliary Power Integrity

Uninterrupted Duty for Critical Substation Control

Engineering parameters designed specifically to feed protection relays, breaker trip circuits, and SCADA automation 24/7.

Dual & Triple Secondary Output Engineering

Substations require multiple distinct secondary voltages to power relay panels (120V AC), station battery chargers (240V AC), and cooling fan bank motors (480V 3-Phase). SKA station transformers feature custom multi-winding secondary configurations in a single compact tank.

IP55 / NEMA 3R Weather-Proof Air Terminal Chambers

Both high and low voltage bushings are enclosed in heavy-duty, gasketed IP55 air terminal boxes with removable gland plates. This protects energized terminals from outdoor weather, ice accumulation, wildlife contact, and accidental human touch.

High Impulse BIL Withstand Ratings

Connected directly to high-voltage substation buses (up to 69 kV class), SKA station transformers are designed with BIL ratings up to 350 kV to absorb lightning surges and switching transients without insulating breakdown.

Complete Remote SCADA Telemetry Interfacing

Integrated with Buchholz gas relays, Pressure Relief Devices (PRD), Magnetic Oil Level Gauges (MOG), and dial Winding/Oil Temperature Indicators (WTI/OTI) wired to a central NEMA 4X marshalling box for continuous remote SCADA monitoring.

Function: Provides a low-reluctance path for magnetic flux. Benefits: Step-lapped CRGO steel minimizes no-load losses and acoustic noise.

Function: Handles oil expansion; Relay detects faults. Benefits: Keeps tank completely full of oil while the relay provides early warning of internal arcing.

Function: Heat dissipation. Benefits: Detachable flanged radiators allow easy maintenance, while optional fans provide ONAF capacity boosts.

Function: Insulates conductors passing through the grounded tank. Benefits: High-creepage porcelain or epoxy ensures zero flashovers in polluted environments.

Function: Adjusts voltage ratios. Benefits: Maintains exact auxiliary voltage even during primary grid fluctuations.

Cooling Systems

Efficient heat dissipation to ensure continuous auxiliary duty.

ONAN (Oil Natural Air Natural)

Working Principle: Oil heated by the core and windings rises naturally to the top of the tank, enters the radiators, cools as it travels downward, and naturally returns to the bottom of the tank.

Cooling Process: Relies entirely on thermodynamic convection and ambient air moving over the radiators.

Advantages: 100% passive, zero maintenance, zero parasitic power loss, highly reliable.

Applications: Standard continuous base-load operation.

ONAF (Oil Natural Air Forced)

Working Principle: Similar to ONAN, but utilizes forced cooling fans mounted on the radiators to dramatically increase the volume of air passing over the fins.

Cooling Process: Sensors trigger the fans when oil temperature reaches a critical setpoint, rapidly dropping temperatures.

Advantages: Temporarily increases the MVA capacity of the transformer by 15% to 33% without increasing physical size.

Applications: Used during peak loads, emergency overload scenarios, or extremely hot ambient conditions.
