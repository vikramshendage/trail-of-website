Dry Type VPI Engineering Philosophy

SKA Vacuum Pressure Impregnated (VPI) Dry-Type Transformers deliver superior electrical, thermal, and mechanical reliability for demanding indoor and environmentally sensitive installations.

Unlike traditional oil-immersed transformers, VPI dry-type units utilize air natural (AN) or air forced (AF) convective cooling. This completely eliminates liquid dielectric fluids, eliminating fire hazards, toxic oil leaks, and soil contamination risks. This makes VPI transformers the ideal solution for commercial high-rises, hospitals, data centers, underground transit, and industrial facilities located close to load centers.

During the manufacturing process, the core-and-coil assembly is placed under deep vacuum to extract trapped air and moisture, then pressure-impregnated with a high-temperature polyester/silicone resin. The unit is thermally cured to form a void-free, moisture-proof, and fire-resistant solid insulation system capable of withstanding extreme thermal cycles and severe short-circuit forces.

Class 220 (Class H & C) High-Temperature Insulation

Engineered with inorganic, non-hygroscopic Nomex® insulation and high-grade resin impregnation, allowing continuous full-load operation at 80°C, 115°C, or 150°C temperature rise over 40°C ambient.

PAGE 05 // CONSTRUCTION DETAILS

Construction & Insulation Details

1. Magnetic Core Assembly

Built using high-permeability, cold-rolled grain-oriented (CRGO) silicon steel. Step-lap mitered joints and boltless clamping reduce magnetic hysteresis losses and operational noise levels.

2. Winding Architecture & GP03 Combs

Windings utilize high-conductivity electrolytic copper. High-voltage coils incorporate custom-fabricated Fiber Glass Reinforced Plastic Grating (GP03) winding combs with carbon fiber tail combs to maintain precise axial cooling ducts and high mechanical tension.

3. VPI Resin Process

The active part undergoes deep vacuum extraction followed by high-pressure polyester resin impregnation. Thermal curing seals all micro-voids, delivering maximum dielectric strength.
