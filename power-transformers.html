What is a Power Transformer?

Our Power Transformers are engineered for reliable, high-efficiency operation in transmission and distribution networks. Designed to handle bulk power transfer at high voltage levels, these transformers are ideal for utility substations, industrial power systems, and power generation facilities.

With voltage ratings up to 132 kV and power ratings ranging from 10 MVA to 150 MVA, our power transformers are built for continuous operation under demanding electrical and environmental conditions. Each unit is custom-designed to meet specific system requirements, ensuring optimal performance, safety, and long service life.

High Voltage Engineering & Grid Resilience

Built to withstand severe grid fault forces, extreme lightning surges, and continuous high-MVA bulk energy transfer.

Continuously Transposed Conductors (CTC)

To eliminate stray eddy current losses in high-current copper windings, SKA power transformers incorporate Continuously Transposed Conductors (CTC). Each individual insulated strand is systematically transposed along the coil length, optimizing electrical efficiency and thermal distribution across all MVA ratings.

MICAFIL Vapour Phase Drying (VPD) Processing

Insulation moisture is the primary enemy of power transformer lifespan. Our core-coil assemblies undergo vacuum drying in a state-of-the-art Vapour Phase Drying plant, reducing insulation moisture levels below 0.5% prior to deep vacuum oil impregnation for long dielectric service life.

120-Ton Hydraulic Coil Clamping & Bracing

During short-circuit faults, electromagnetic forces try to rip transformer coils apart. SKA power transformer coils are thermally pre-compressed under a 120-ton hydraulic press and locked with pressboard clamping rings, ensuring complete dimensional stability against short-circuit forces.

On-Load Tap Changer (OLTC) Voltage Tuning

Equipped with motor-driven On-Load Tap Changers (OLTC) paired with automatic voltage regulators (AVR), SKA power transformers continuously maintain stable secondary busbar voltage without interrupting load current during grid voltage fluctuations.

Construction Details

Engineering breakdown of a High Voltage Power Transformer

High-grade CRGO silicon steel, step-lapped and tightly bound to minimize magnetic losses and noise.

Electrolytic copper/aluminum windings structurally designed for superior dielectric strength and short-circuit withstand capabilities.

Robust, leak-proof welded mild steel tank capable of holding thousands of liters of oil under full vacuum and positive pressure.

Detachable fin-type radiators paired with automated forced air fans (ONAF) to dissipate massive heat generation.

Expansion tank located above the main tank to absorb oil volume changes due to temperature fluctuations.

Porcelain or composite insulation allowing high voltage conductors to safely pass through the grounded tank.

Enables voltage regulation without interrupting the load current, essential for transmission stability.

Buchholz relay detects internal gas accumulation. Pressure Relief Devices safely vent rapid pressure spikes.

OTI and WTI monitor thermal limits. The Marshalling Box acts as the central hub for all control and alarm wiring.

Cooling Systems

High capacity thermal dissipation methodologies.

ONAN

Oil Natural Air Natural. Standard convective cooling relying on radiator fins.

ONAF

Oil Natural Air Forced. Adding cooling fans to increase capacity by up to 33%.

OFAF

Oil Forced Air Forced. Pumps circulate oil while fans force air over radiators.

ODAF

Oil Directed Air Forced. Oil is directly pumped into the winding ducts for massive MVA units.
