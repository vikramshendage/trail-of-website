PAGE 02 // PAD MOUNTED OVERVIEW

Underground Power Distribution Solutions

SKA Pad-Mounted Transformers are ground-installed, liquid-filled distribution transformers securely housed inside a locked, tamper-resistant steel cabinet mounted on an outdoor concrete pad.

Designed exclusively for underground electrical distribution circuits, pad-mounted transformers eliminate unsightly overhead utility poles and wire clutter. Because all high and low voltage connections are completely enclosed in a grounded, tamper-proof compartment, pad-mounted transformers can be safely installed in public spaces without protective fencing.

Available in single-phase (up to 167 kVA) and three-phase (up to 5000 kVA) designs up to 34.5 kV primary class, SKA pad-mounted units comply strictly with CSA C227.4, IEEE C57.12.26, IEEE C57.12.28 (Tamper Resistance), and DOE efficiency standards.

Dead-Front & Live-Front Configurations

Dead-front designs feature insulated, touch-safe separable elbow connectors for maximum operator safety. Live-front options offer porcelain bushings for specific utility preferences.

Cabinet Construction & Components

1. Heavy-Gauge Steel Enclosure

Fabricated from heavy mild steel with a recessed sill, captive penta-head locking bolt, and padlocking hasp meeting IEEE C57.12.28 tamper resistance requirements.

2. Dead-Front HV Bushing Wells

High-voltage epoxy bushing wells accept 200A loadbreak or non-loadbreak separable elbow connectors, ensuring completely touch-safe operator protection.

3. Internal Core-Coil Assembly

Step-lap CRGO steel core with copper/aluminum windings, dried under vacuum and submerged in high-grade mineral or FR3 natural ester dielectric fluid.
