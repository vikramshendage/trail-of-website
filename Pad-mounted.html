What is a Pad Mounted Transformer?

Underground Power Distribution

A pad-mounted transformer is a ground-mounted electrical distribution transformer in a locked steel cabinet mounted on a concrete pad. Since all energized connection points are securely enclosed within a grounded metal housing, they can be installed in places that do not have room for a fenced enclosure, ensuring maximum public safety.

Unlike traditional pole-mounted transformers, pad-mounted units are designed exclusively for underground distribution lines. This eliminates unsightly overhead cables, making them the standard choice for modern residential communities, commercial complexes, and urban infrastructure.

Dead-Front vs. Live-Front Designs
  • Dead-Front: Utilizes insulated, touch-safe separable elbow connectors. This design ensures maximum safety for operators and the public, as no exposed high-voltage parts are present when the cabinet is opened.
  • Live-Front: Features exposed porcelain or epoxy bushings. While requiring more stringent safety protocols during maintenance, it offers distinct operational advantages in specific utility networks.

Advantages Over Pole-Mounted

  • No visual pollution from overhead lines.
  • Eliminates weather-related outages (falling trees, ice storms).
  • Easier ground-level access for maintenance crews.
  • Higher KVA capacities available.
  • Significantly safer for surrounding public spaces.

Tamper-Proof Safety & Underground Power Distribution

Discreet, touch-safe, weather-resistant transformers engineered for public residential spaces and solar farms.

IEEE C57.12.28 Tamper-Resistant Cabinet Security

SKA pad-mounted transformers feature heavy steel cabinets with recessed door sills, overlapping door edges, captive penta-head locking bolts, and padlocking handles. Certified to IEEE C57.12.28 tamper resistance standards for safe installation in public parks and residential neighborhoods.

Dead-Front Touch-Safe Separable Elbow Connectors

High-voltage connections utilize epoxy bushing wells and 200A loadbreak/non-loadbreak insulated separable elbow connectors. This dead-front design ensures zero exposed energized parts, guaranteeing safety during switching operations and line maintenance.

FR3 Bio-Degradable Natural Ester Fluid Option

Available with Envirotemp™ FR3™ natural ester dielectric fluid. FR3 features a high fire point (360°C), self-extinguishing properties, and complete bio-degradability—eliminating toxic oil leak hazards near residential areas and waterways.

Dual Bay-O-Net & ELSP Current-Limiting Backup Fusing

Protection is provided by externally removable Bay-O-Net oil-immersed fuses in series with ELSP current-limiting backup fuses. This dual fusing arrangement protects the grid against internal high-current faults and low-current secondary overloads.

Construction Details

An inside look at our robust engineering.

Fully sealed, welded carbon steel tank housing a high-efficiency CRGO step-lap core designed to minimize no-load losses.

Precision wound Copper or Aluminum coils securely braced to withstand severe short-circuit forces.

Encloses all HV and LV compartments with a multi-point Door Locking System, meeting IEEE C57.12.28 tamper resistance standards.

Dead-front units feature universal bushing wells and inserts. LV side utilizes robust spade terminals for massive current loads.

High-dielectric mineral oil or FR3 fluid circulates through corrugated cooling fins/radiators for exceptional heat dissipation.

Dual-stage protection utilizing Bay-O-Net fuses for overload/secondary faults and Current Limiting Fuses (ELSP) for high-current primary faults.

Automatic PRD to vent dangerous pressure build-ups. Standard 1" drain valve with sampling device included.

Copper-faced steel Ground Pads for solid earthing. Externally operable Off-Circuit Tap Changer for voltage regulation.

Protection Systems

  • Bay-O-Net Fuse: Draw-out expulsion fuse providing protection against secondary faults and severe overloads.
  • Current Limiting Fuse: Backup protection to isolate primary system faults before tank rupture can occur.
  • Pressure Relief Device: Automatically vents excessive internal pressure.
  • Grounding System: Solid external copper-faced pads to bond the tank to earth ground.
  • Arc Resistant Design: Minimizes blast impact during catastrophic internal failure.
  • Tamper Resistant Cabinet: Penta-head bolts and padlock provisions secure the dead-front / lockable doors against unauthorized access.
  • Monitoring: Oil Level Monitoring and Temperature Protection indicators available.
