PAGE 02 // STATION OVERVIEW

Critical Substation Support Infrastructure

SKA Station Transformers (Station Service / Auxiliary Transformers) provide reliable, continuous low-voltage auxiliary power to utility substations and power generation plants.

Station transformers step down high voltage from the main transmission bus (up to 69 kV) to lower operational voltages (such as 480V, 240V, or 120V) to feed the substation's critical internal systems—including protection relay panels, circuit breaker trip coils, battery chargers, cooling fan/pump motors, station lighting, and SCADA control automation.

Because a loss of auxiliary power can compromise the entire substation's protection and control integrity, SKA Station Transformers are custom-engineered for maximum short-circuit strength, zero-downtime reliability, and extreme environmental durability under IEEE C57.12.00 and CSA C88 standards.

Mission-Critical Uninterrupted Duty

Engineered with low temperature rise options (55°C/65°C), high BIL impulse withstand, and heavy-duty steel tanks for continuous 24/7 service in utility grids worldwide.

PAGE 05 // CONSTRUCTION DETAILS

Construction & Component Engineering

1. Core Construction

Precision step-lap mitered core built from prime CRGO silicon steel laminations, clamped with high-tensile glass bands to minimize excitation losses and vibration noise.

2. Windings & Terminal Chambers

High-conductivity copper windings braced against axial and radial short-circuit forces. HV & LV bushings are housed within IP55 weather-proof terminal boxes.

3. Tank & Accessories

Heavy welded mild steel tank equipped with detachable radiators, Buchholz relay, pressure relief valve, magnetic oil level gauge, and NEMA 3R marshalling box.
