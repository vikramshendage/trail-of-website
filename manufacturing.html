Transformer Manufacturing Process

From Raw Materials to Reliable Power Solutions

Timeline

The 15-Step Transformer Journey

Click "Expand Details" on any card to explore the technical parameters, engineering specifications, and quality controls of each manufacturing stage.

Step 1: Engineering & Design

Precise review of specifications and design optimization using electromagnetic software.

  • Customer requirement analysis & technical specification review.
  • Electrical and mechanical design calculations for windings, core size, and loss limits.
  • Preparation of detailed General Arrangement (GA) and structural drawings.
  • Design validation (thermal and electrical stress analysis) using Finite Element Method (FEM) software.
Step 2: Raw Material Procurement

Inspection and procurement of high-grade raw materials from certified global suppliers.

Incoming Materials Include:

  • CRGO / Amorphous Core Steel
  • Electrolytic Copper
  • Aluminium Conductors
  • Transformer Oil (FR3/Mineral)
  • Insulation Paper (Nomex/Kraft)
  • Pressboard & Epoxy Resin
  • High Voltage Bushings
  • Off-circuit / On-load Tap Changers
  • Radiators & Tank Steel Plates
  • Conservators & Cooling Accessories

All materials undergo incoming quality inspection (IQC) tests before release.

Step 3: Core Manufacturing

Cutting and stacking core laminations using step-lap joints to minimize eddy currents.

  • CRGO sheet inspection and slitting.
  • Step-lap cutting and automatic deburring.
  • Careful manual/automatic stacking of laminations.
  • Core clamping and core loss testing to verify low no-load losses.
Step 4: Coil Winding

Winding low-voltage and high-voltage conductors on CNC machines.

  • LV coil winding & HV coil winding under controlled tension.
  • Selection of Winding Types: Foil, Disc, Helical, or Layer winding based on voltage/current class.
  • Installation of inter-layer insulation and radial cooling ducts.
  • Hydraulic coil pressing to ensure compact structural height.
Step 5: Insulation Process

Structuring insulation barriers to withstand electrical and environmental stress.

  • Preparation and assembly of pressboard components and insulating cylinders.
  • Installation of spacer blocks, end rings, and barrier insulation.
  • Meticulous insulation inspection to verify clearance spacing.
Step 6: Core & Coil Assembly

Mounting wound coils over core limbs and executing primary electrical links.

  • Mounting HV and LV coils over stacked core limbs.
  • Core yoke assembly, clamping, and mechanical tightening.
  • Routing leads, soldering contacts, and wiring tap-changer connections.
Step 7: Drying Process

Extracting microscopic moisture from active parts inside vacuum ovens.

Moisture compromises dielectric strength, triggering partial discharges. We apply:

  • Pre-heating cycle in temperature-controlled ovens.
  • Deep vacuum drying to pull out moisture molecules from core-coil blocks.
  • Electronic moisture measurements and continuous temperature logging.
Step 8: VPI (For Dry-Type Units)

Impregnating polyester resin under pressure for air-cooled transformers.

9-Step VPI Process:

  1. Loading assembly into vacuum chamber.
  2. Air evacuation (vacuum pull).
  3. Resin tank filling.
  4. Pressure impregnation to force resin.
  5. Resin penetration hold time.
  6. Resin drainage.
  7. Curing cycle inside baking ovens.
  8. Controlled cooling.
  9. Final visual and insulation inspection.

Benefits: Better insulation, higher mechanical strength, moisture resistance, and longer service life.

Step 9: Tank Fabrication

Fabricating structural steel tanks to hold liquid-cooled active parts.

  • CNC bending and steel plate laser cutting.
  • Precise welding of structural columns and corrugations.
  • Pressure testing tank welds for leak-proof sealing.
  • Surface grinding to eliminate welding seams.
Step 10: Surface Prep & Painting

Applying multi-stage coatings to protect tanks from environmental corrosion.

  • Automatic steel shot-blasting to clean surfaces.
  • Application of zinc-rich anti-corrosive primer.
  • Epoxy wet painting or electrostatic powder coating.
  • Oven baking to seal coat thickness and dry-film inspection.
Step 11: Final Assembly

Mounting active parts into tanks and wiring all external auxiliary devices.

Accessories Mounted Include:

  • Bushing insulators
  • Radiators & Conservator
  • Cooling fans
  • Buchholz & Pressure relays
  • Marshalling box & wiring
  • Breathers & Oil temp indicators
  • Winding temperature sensors
  • Name plate & Earthing lugs
  • Substructure wheels
Step 12: Oil Filling & Filtration

Filling fluid under deep vacuum followed by fine-particle oil filtration.

  • Evacuating tank air before introducing insulation fluid.
  • Vacuum oil filling to eliminate residual bubbles inside winding structures.
  • Multi-stage degassing, oil filtration, and moisture-absorption cycles.
  • Oil dielectric strength testing (breakdown voltage limits).
Step 13: Testing & QA

Running extensive laboratory tests to check performance against global standards.

Routine Tests:

  • Insulation Resistance
  • Turns Ratio & Phase
  • Winding Resistance
  • Vector Group check
  • Load & No-Load Loss
  • Applied/Induced Voltage

Type Tests:

  • Temperature Rise
  • Lightning Impulse
  • Short Circuit withstand

Special Tests:

  • Partial Discharge
  • Sound Level test
  • Harmonic Analysis
  • FRA & Tan Delta

Complies with IEC 60076, IEEE C57, IS 2026, and customer specifications.

Step 14: Final Inspection

Verification of dimensions, paint thickness, and final documentation.

  • Mechanical check: checking tap selector settings, oil gauges, valve operations.
  • Dimensional verification matching GA layout specifications.
  • Documentation verification: assembly records, test reports, and certificate books.
  • Optional customer witness or third-party inspection (SGS, BV).
Step 15: Packing & Dispatch

Protective packaging, crate mounting, and logistics loading for shipping.

  • Cleaning, drying, and protective plastic shrink wrapping.
  • Heavy-duty wooden packing/crating for structural support.
  • Container loading and secure transport tie-downs.
  • Preparing customs, shipping, and export documentation for dispatch.
Standards

International Grid Specifications

Every transformer is engineered and manufactured to strict international utility and industrial standards.

CSA Standards

Full compliance with Canadian Standards Association specifications (including CSA C2.1, CSA C88, and CSA C301) for utility distribution and substation transformers operating on the Canadian grid.

IEEE / ANSI Standards

Adhering strictly to IEEE C57 series standards, ensuring absolute safety, thermal efficiency, short-circuit withstand performance, and electrical insulation coordination.

CAN National Standards

Compliant with Canadian National Standards (CAN/CSA) and international specifications for distribution, power, and air-cooled dry-type transformer installations.

TESTING LABORATORY

Stringent Quality Assurance

Every transformer is subjected to rigorous electrical and mechanical testing in our NABL-accredited high-voltage laboratory.

Routine Tests

Measurement of voltage ratio, winding resistance, impedance voltage, load loss, and no-load loss in accordance with IEC 60076, CSA/CAN, and IEEE standards.

Type Tests

Temperature rise tests and lightning impulse tests to verify the design integrity under extreme grid conditions.

Partial Discharge

High-sensitivity PD measurement ensures flawless insulation integrity and guarantees long operational life without degradation.

Special Tests

Acoustic sound level measurement, zero-sequence impedance, and short-circuit withstand capabilities upon request.
